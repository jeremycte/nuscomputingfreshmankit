FOW OComm Essay Top Picks #1

FOW, well what can I say, asking me about my experience in FOW is like asking a software engineer to code out a program to find the last element of a linked list. He/ She probably knows it, prepared days for it but alas no code on the IDE (pre SOC trauma pls send help). I’ll try my best to pen down the rollercoaster of emotions I’ve felt in this short span of time. Day 1 started with me not knowing how zoom worked despite a manual being sent out beforehand (excuse my lazy self). After I got the hang of it, I was introduced to my OG. At first, we were all silent and acted awkward because that’s what humans do. But we started warming up to each other talking about shared experience, the bell curve and how we would come to worship it, how COVID is the mother of all disasters and so on bonding over the things that unite us.

This was followed by a series of talks by the sponsors of this camp. The information presented was eye opening and got me thinking about many things I didn’t consider important in the past. However, I am also a multitasking god so I tried to complete a task like the one I’m doing now. It took me a while but I coded out the INCIPIO logo on the terminal. When I saw the logo pop up on the screen I was exhilarated. One must know that my programming skills are rudimentary so to achieve anything even remotely technical was a win for my deprived brain. Moving on to speed networking. This was a segment I enjoyed. I had meaningful conversations with my fellow roommates. Topics of top tier importance such as, “Which Hall are you staying in?”, “Do you have a GF?”, “How to get a GF?” were discussed in great detail. I had a great laugh and a fun time talking to new people.

Moving forward to day 2 which had more games, YAY interaction! who was I kidding my introverted self was in turmoil. But as Bear Grylls says “Improvise, Adapt, overcome” I took his words to heart and put myself out there participating avidly in the games of the morning. The OGL’s did make it easier for introverts like me to feel comfortable and participate (definitely wasn’t paid to say this). The first game involved some drawing where I made a complete fool of myself. However, I redeemed myself by using me ‘among us’ training to prevent getting caught as the spy, fun times. Moving forward with the item search a game which made me rack my brains then realize how stupid I was, definitely had me questioning if I was worthy enough to go to SOC but its ok, IT IS WHAT IT IS. Now presenting a skit with these items, that was a challenge.

My team came up with a good story linking all the items together and gave it a naughty ending. Presenting said skit resulted in a sea of laughter, the only thing that made it better was that the opposing team was on par with their skit leaving the OCCOM to choose the lesser of the 2 evils, definitely one of the more hilarious segments. Then came Clash of titans, I took the role of defending cityguessr which was fun because somehow my team was really good at it. However, it was a challenging game which pushed the limits of my general knowledge and geography. Overall, it was a hilarious and fun journey filled with ingots of information along the way. In my opinion FOW has raised the bar for what an online camp can achieve and the camp hasn’t even ended so props to everyone involved in making this camp a success. Expecting more fun on day 3. Peace.

- Geolog 1

FOW OComm Essay Top Picks #2

Coming into Freshmen Orientation Week, we did not know what to expect. With pandemic measures tightening by the week, there was much doubt on whether this camp will still proceed as usual. Yet, those uncertainties cleared up quickly as we entered our first Zoom room. Under the calming voice of Abhishek (like a professional sports announcer), we quickly settled into our respective OG groups in an orderly manner. We strongly appreciate Computing Club’s efforts in hosting this Freshmen Orientation Week despite the sudden changes in COVID-19 measures. Despite the challenges that the new lockdown measures bring, we are grateful that we are still able to have fun and meet new friends on Zoom. It has been really fun and I’ve met a lot of new and fun people! The OGLs were really nice and we appreciate the efforts of the OCOMM for putting in the effort to replan the whole camp. We also really appreciate the effort that the game masters put, trying their best to make the games as entertaining as possible!

The amount and variety of activities during this camp took us by surprise. From sponsor sharings to Mod Reg talks to exciting (and physically tiring) Zoom games, friendships were definitely forged after this intense 3 day camp. In particular, I enjoyed the Board Game on Day 3 because they allowed us to participate more physically. It was very engaging because of it’s high intensity and competition. I also really liked that it allowed us to come together as an OG and work together to play a game as a group.

Besides the friendships forged, it was cool to see all the talented freshies in the camp. So many people can sing like Adele smh. Then got one guy can change shirt while being upside down sheeeeshhh. We witnessed mind blowing magic tricks and heard people making water droplet sounds?! Seriously, there are some true hidden talents around SoC ._. It feels amazing to be in a group with these secret talents! But at the same time it shows how untalented I am LOL. Hopefully these 4 years in NUS will give me opportunities to discover my talents!!! It was amazing to meet all these people online as well!

From listening to the Mod Reg talks, I gained many new insights towards how to plan my timetable. It was genuinely helpful to hear our seniors going through the process step-by-step through their own ModReg platform! The advices that they gave were very helpful and helped me in my process of deciding what modules to bid for.

Furthermore, this camp also gives us an insight on how exciting NUS life will be! With the insightful modreg talk held by our seniors, I felt so reassured and confident that I will be able to tackle university life. During the start of the camp, I thought of just leaving. But through the fun filled activities, I realise there was so much more planned for us. I was like… OMG this camp is legit so freaking amazing… IS THIS EVEN ONLINE? I FEEL SO CONNECTED TO THE PEOPLE AROUND ME. I WAS LIKE WOW THIS CAMP IS 10/10 *FIRE EMOJI*. In particular was the 1st day, when we went to Vivo to meet up with our OGL & OGmates. I never felt so connected to my peers. Feeling very very very very excited to start school. Except for CS1010J. I think I’m going to cry, my seniors say it’s already the most chill 1010 mod, but when you hear about it…. Wah I think maybe I should have gone to CHS… but then I was so inspired by the MODREG talk when my seniors hyped me telling me it’s manageable one la! So sheeeeeshhhh lets GET IT!! SEND IT MY DUDE! LEGGOOOOOO. On a more serious note, I would like to thank my OGL from DYRE. I think it’s not easy leading a bunch of strangers through an online camp! So yeah!! YOU THE BEST BROOOO SHHEEESHHH SEND IT !!!!!

FOW has given me the opportunity to meet many people in Computing. Through this camp, I managed to bond and interact with people. Despite the COVID restrictions, I have enjoyed the activities planned. The committee members and OGL have made the best out of the situation. I would like to thank all the committee members and OGL for their effort during these 3 days. The advice the seniors have given us is also useful for our future University life and I would like to show my appreciation for that. Hope I will be able to see and say hi to everyone else in school.

All in all, DRUM ROLLLSSSS PLEASEEEEE DU DU DU DU DU DU DUUUUUU DUUUUUUUU SOC FOW FOR DA WINNNNNN OCOMM best comm!!!! JIAN XIANG BEST BEST BEST BEST BESTEST BOY!!!!! JIAN XIANG is amazing, devastating, electrifying, talented, brilliant, incredible, humorous, spectacular, show stopping, best OGL on Earth WOOO HOOOOO!! Thank you seniors for planning and re-planning such LITTT LIT LIT LITTTT camp. y’all da best uwu because i got to meet friends from BARGGGG and EBRUUU!! my new friends are damn cool and we made fun memories together UWUU going to sentosa, eating dinner with so many bugs attacking us and sand flying into our faces and food RIPS BUT ALLS GOOD then we went vivo city to talk talk walk walk vibe vibe COOL STUFF and got merchandise also my OGL say the merch are damn good.

Besides the gift, I think is really really well planned and I feel very grateful that there are really awesome seniors who have put in effort to make my freshmen camp the very best ever. I am so touched, think I need 5 seconds to cry. Okay.. I’m done crying but seriously I’m really super touched by everything!

Freshman Orientation Week honestly exceeded my expectations so much for a camp that had to be turned into an online camp at the last minute. I was expecting a lot of awkward planning but O’Comm managed to put together a really comprehensive and fun camp that had a good balance of games and activities, as well as more official talks and information sharing sessions.

SIDENOTE: SOC FOW LEGIT BEST CAMP, I LOVE KAUSHIkK I WILL MISS MY TIME AT FOW 2021, NOTHING CAN BEAT THIS AMAZING CAMP BECAUSE ALL THE OGLS, HELPERS, FRESHIES, OCOMM, PDs are the best in the world. BEST 4EVA, NOTHING WILL EVER TOP THIS EVER! FOW FTW LETS GO

FOW OComm Essay Top Picks #3

Before FOW, we were apprehensive about meeting new people and the complex module system in NUS. However, the Ocomm did a good job publicising the camp on social media platforms and hyped up the camp before it happened. During FOW, we played a lot of games like haxball, agario, codenames and 2truths1lie. We even performed a skit based on frozen.

We also got to meet many new friends during the speed networking sessions. Although it was a brief meeting, we made many new friends, who we can probably reach out to even after the camp to connect better. It was a goo opportunity to find other people staying in the same residences or part of the same majors outside of our own group. The FOW committee also took their time to create a zoom meeting session to brief freshies from the School of Computing on the module registration and to host a question and answer session for us which was literally the greatest thing ever. They actually guided us clueless freshies through the entire process of modreg and patiently answered all of our questions, even though we may have repeatedly asked the same questions. I think without the talk a lot of us would still have been very lost. The organiser for the CS modreg talk was also very helpful and answered all the questions, even when the talk stretched way past the end time until 10+.

Abhishek conducted our SOC circuit game and he was a GREAT host! He played really good music and helped to commentate on the videos showing us around school of computing, which really made it seem like an in real life school tour! So special shoutout to him because even before FOW, he was very quick to answer our queries on both the SOC freshmen chat and the BZA chat, and gave us very helpful answers to our questions :D We also really enjoyed the BBB organised by Rish and Megan. We thought it was very well planned out and the questions really helped to stimulate our brains HAHAHA. It was so exciting to solve each question and check whether we can progress to the next level or not. The storyline of FOW was also another highlight as we managed to witness the hidden talents of the OComm, House Heads and Lord Kaushik.

Now that we are coming to the end of FOW, we are grateful for all the fun experiences and games that have bonded our OG together like no other camp in NUS could! Although it was a huge setback that we were unable to have a physical camp, the OGLs and OComm managed to make the atmosphere as hyped as it would be for an in person FOW. Coupled together with the great prizes prepared and the SOCash system, it allowed us to break out of our comfort zone to make great friends even through a screen :D

We will continue to be friends even after the camp, and we will form study groups and take mods together and go through the rest of our time in SoC together. We will suffer together, help each other, and celebrate each others’ victories. We are also grateful for steph for taking time to be an ogl I mean she could have been having fun at home HAHAH so yea THANKu steph for being a great and nice ogl:D

Trewitt best house!!!! Denise best house head!!!! Penguin best OG!!!!!! Steph best OGL!!! Roy Abhishek and Lakshminarayanan Kaushik Rangaraj are the best PD and VPDs!! Weilin, Qing Yi, Jia Ming, and Jonathan are the best pubs comm!!! Jingya, Madhan, Sophie, and Tze Kang are the best logs comm!!! Brandon, Guan Wei, Michelle, Nobel, and Brendan are the best admin comm!!! Rish, Yu Xian, Megan, Sue Ann, Jeremy, Dennis, Zhe Kai, Wen Xin, Bryan, and Minh Hai are the best progs comm!!!! FOW BEST CAMP WOOHOOOOOOo

FOW OComm Essay Top Picks #4

Hmmm well describe my experience in FOW in 500 words huh? Well, let's just say in the beginning I was really very very sad that the FOW was going to be changed from a hybrid one to a completely virtual one. And just nice the Phase Two Heightened Alert was announced just a day or two before. Furthermore, a lot of my friends from national service and my previous junior college did not manage to join this orientation programme. This combined with the fact that it was to be fully virtual, made me feel initially that FOW would not be very fun and so I won’t really be able to to learn more things and make new friends. However, to my surprise,this was totally not true. I also have to say that I am thoroughly impressed by the planning committee’s ability to plan all the virtual games and activities etc on the spot.

For example, there was a section where we could bid for games to play within our OG or against other OGs which was quite cool as I didn't know such games existed beforehand. Also, I would say that my favourite game would be the one where we had to draw stuff and other people had to guess what we had drawn. The game was quite fun as we could see how the miscommunication can lead to misconstrued words, such as a caterpillar being interpreted as a “grasshopper becoming a cocoon”. To me personally, this is honestly a life lesson as we need to realise that in a real life setting be it in university, at home or next time in the working world, sometimes what we say might be misrepresented and not communicated accurately and effectively. So I would like to thank the management committee for those really fun and interactive orientation with such a short notice and with so many challenges along the way.

Next, I would like to talk about my house, Geolog, which is the best house in FOW! Geolog is the house of intelligence, and clearly we can see that from the way we do our tasks: full of brain, wit and just enough brawn. We are going to be the CS gods and we are going to whack every other House’s back. And we certainly don't need the bell curve god as we gonna ace all our mods hehe.

So, after talking about the best house in FOW, Geolog, now let me talk about the best OG, OG 6 Skynet!! So skynet has seven wonderful freshmen and an awesome OGL Elijah! Everyone here has their own talents and merits, and we work together to form a wonderful team! Our strengths overcome each other's weaknesses and vice versa. We managed to display our togetherness and bonding many times through games and team building activities, and managed to slowly open up to each other and build up a team. It was a quick 3 days, and I have to say we certainly formed strong lasting bonds with our OG mates through the fast, effective yet fun activities planned for us! I would like to thank everyone who made this possible!

- Geolog 6

FOW OComm Essay Top Picks #6

FOW has been a great experience for each and every one of us Barg freshies. It has certainly allowed us to make a lot of new friends through the bonding games. We are extremely grateful for all the effort and hard work that the organising committee has put in. Even though the new measures were only announced at the very last minute to have the camp entirely online, the committee has certainly done a great job in quickly adapting to the new measures in order to allow freshies to still have a great time. This was in addition to the grief and disappointment they must have felt when they first saw the news on that day. Not only did they quickly accept the new measures and lockdown scenario / the new circular form Office of Student Admissions, they overcame their grief and disappointment and put all their time and effort in to producing a satisfactory camp for the freshies.

FOW has allowed me the opportunity to bond with others in the School of Computing. I was able to play fun and engaging on line games from the various activities planned by the O com over the past 2 days and get to better know my orientation group and talk with other people. I especially enjoyed the find the leader game where we had to be extremely observant to the other players in an opponent house in order to identify who was starting the action chain. This allowed me to have an active time doing actions and laughing together with my orientation group friends as well as the opponent team’s freshies. It was especially enjoyable as the it was our defending event and our Orientation group leader Vernice was an excellent conducting Orientation group leader who managed to keep all of us engaged and competitive throughout all 3 rounds of the games which we played. Without fail, at every round, she kept us on our toes, celebrating victory with us when we triumphed and comforting us when we were disappointed and wallowed in defeat. She alone motivated us to give our best for the games and kept us pushing for greater heights.

FOW has introduced me to what life in NUS SoC is like, learning from our seniors' experiences and listening to their advice. It has also been an avenue to see what others expect and to re-evaluate my own expectations. It is a bit sad that the experience has been forced online by COVID, but it is nonetheless a good first look into the life to expect, and to meet people that will follow me for the next few years. I hope that the lighthearted nature of this camp will set the right tone for my batchmates and remind us that we are all going through the same challenges together. I also hope that we will act as a nice support structure for each other to ask for help when we get stuck debugging.

FOW is a very refreshing camp as it has been very long since I attended a camp. It has provided me a good opportunity to find new friends who are attending the same course or are in the same faculty. While it is definitely heart-wrenching that the camp has to go online due to the recent outbreak, I'm thankful for the ocomm for planning out this camp with so many changes along the way. I am also thankful for my OGL and house head, Vernice and Kian , for trying their best to bring out everyone’s enthusiasm and being a good senior to guide us in our new university life.

First of all, this is an amazing opportunity to talk to new people and have a solid group of friends to kickstart our university life. Computing is not an easy course and we certainly need a good group of friends as a solid support system to get through these few years of hard university life

Last of all, I would like to thank the amazing PDs and VPDs Roy, Kaushik and Abhishek, OComm and House Heads and OGLs for taking their precious time during the summer break and even starting as early as last December to plan this camp for us. Although things did not turn out the way we wanted, hopefully we can meet up again once covid restrictions get better! Thank you to hsiao ting zhao wei and raveen for providing endless support for the camp. We are eternally forever grateful. May someone/something bless us from this deadly pandemic and lift us out of the situation so that future batches will never have to experience such adversities ever again. Peace and love only.

- BARG 3 & 4

I was definitely disheartened when we were told that both the physical days were cancelled just the night before orientation. Before coming into the camp, I had the impression that it would be boring and things would be awkward, however, it was nothing like I imagined it to be. I am glad everyone was participative and enthusiastic with the games!! Kudos to the ocomm members and ogl for planning months in advance and also being really hyped throughout the camp despite the situation. It was a fun and wholesome camp with lots of laughter! Everyone was really friendly and open throughout the camp, which really helped to break the ice! Thanks for a good start to my SOC journey:)

BARG 2 Freshie
